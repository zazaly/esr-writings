## []{#shell-tools}Master Foo and the Shell Tools {#master-foo-and-the-shell-tools .title style="clear: both"}

</div>

</div>
:::::

A Unix novice came to Master Foo and said: ["[I am confused. Is it not the Unix way that every program should concentrate on one thing and do it well?]{.quote}"]{.quote}

Master Foo nodded.

The novice continued: ["[Isn\'t it also the Unix way that the wheel should not be reinvented?]{.quote}"]{.quote}

Master Foo nodded again.

["[Why, then, are there several tools with similar capabilities in text processing: sed, awk and Perl? With which one can I best practice the Unix way?]{.quote}"]{.quote}

Master Foo asked the novice: ["[If you have a text file, what tool would you use to produce a copy with a few words in it replaced by strings of your choosing?]{.quote}"]{.quote}

The novice frowned and said: ["[Perl\'s regexps would be excessive for so simple a task. I do not know awk, and I have been writing sed scripts in the last few weeks. As I have some experience with sed, at the moment I would prefer it. But if the job only needed to be done once rather than repeatedly, a text editor would suffice.]{.quote}"]{.quote}

Master Foo nodded and replied: ["[When you are hungry, eat; when you are thirsty, drink; when you are tired, sleep.]{.quote}"]{.quote}

Upon hearing this, the novice was enlightened.
