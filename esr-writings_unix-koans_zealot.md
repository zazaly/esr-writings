## []{#zealot}Master Foo and the Unix Zealot {#master-foo-and-the-unix-zealot .title style="clear: both"}

</div>

</div>
:::::

A Unix zealot, having heard that Master Foo was wise in the Great Way, came to him for instruction. Master Foo said to him:

["[When the Patriarch Thompson invented Unix, he did not understand it. Then he gained in understanding, and no longer invented it.]{.quote}"]{.quote}

["[When the Patriarch McIlroy invented the pipe, he knew that it would transform software, but did not know that it would transform mind.]{.quote}"]{.quote}

["[When the Patriarch Ritchie invented C, he condemned programmers to a thousand hells of buffer overruns, heap corruption, and stale-pointer bugs.]{.quote}"]{.quote}

["[Truly, the Patriarchs were blind and foolish!]{.quote}"]{.quote}

The zealot was greatly angered by the Master\'s words.

["[These enlightened ones,]{.quote}"]{.quote} he protested, ["[gave us the Great Way of Unix. Surely, if we mock them we will lose merit and be reborn as beasts or MCSEs.]{.quote}"]{.quote}

["[Is your code ever completely without stain and flaw?]{.quote}"]{.quote} demanded Master Foo.

["[No,]{.quote}"]{.quote} admitted the zealot, ["[no man\'s is.]{.quote}"]{.quote}

["[The wisdom of the Patriarchs]{.quote}"]{.quote} said Master Foo, ["[was that they [*knew*]{.emphasis} they were fools.]{.quote}"]{.quote}

Upon hearing this, the zealot was enlightened.
